Transition History

This is a detailed (and somewhat chronological) record of the steps I’ve taken throughout my transition. It’s intentionally factual, but reflects a long and ongoing journey.

2021 — Getting Started

In September 2021, I met with my primary care physician and shared that I wanted to transition. She referred me to endocrinology.

By October, I met with an endocrinologist, but many of my questions—especially around electrolysis and hair transplants—remained unanswered. I was referred to dermatology for further guidance.

In November, I had a dermatology visit. While not specialized in transition-related care, I was given a short list of hair transplant surgeons. After some research, I selected Dr. Berman as the best fit.

2022 — First Major Steps

In January 2022, I underwent my first hair transplant (3,000 grafts). Around the same time, I began laser hair removal across my entire body.

By May, laser treatments were effective on my arms and legs, but not on my face. I began electrolysis for facial hair, initially committing to 2-hour sessions five to six times per week.

As progress continued, I reduced electrolysis to four sessions per week by October.

2023 — Continued Progress

In February 2023, I had a second hair transplant (1,000 grafts).

Electrolysis gradually decreased as progress improved:

  • July: reduced to 3 sessions per week
  • October: reduced to 2 sessions per week

2024 — Medical & Hormonal Changes

In January 2024, I began Finasteride and topical Minoxidil.

In April 2024, I started Estradiol and transitioned from Finasteride to Dutasteride.

By June 2024, electrolysis had been reduced to once per week (still two-hour sessions).

In December 2024, I had a third hair transplant (2,200 grafts, performed by Dr. Varona), which was covered by insurance. Around this time, I also began Progesterone and reached Tanner Stage 3.

2025–2026 — Continued Growth

In the summer of 2025, I continued to see significant physical and personal changes as my transition progressed. My hair continued to grow and thicken, and my breasts filled out to about a 40 C/D!

In the late winter and spring of 2026, with the shedding of winter layers, I’ve been able to go out in lighter clothing and live more comfortably day to day, with no makeup, wig, or breastforms. I continue to move through everyday activities—running errands, walking in my neighborhood, and just existing in the world—while still carrying the familiar doubts that come with being transgender. Even so, my day-to-day experience feels more natural and aligned than it ever has before.

While the path hasn’t been easy, I’ve reached a point of greater comfort, confidence, and continuity in how I experience myself day to day.

Summary

  • Hair Transplants: 3 procedures totaling 6,200 grafts
  • Electrolysis: Approximately 800 hours (and ongoing need)
